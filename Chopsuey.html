Chopsuey

Chopsuey is a popular Filipino stir-fried vegetable dish made with a mix of fresh vegetables, meat, and shrimp in a savory sauce. This colorful and nutritious dish is a staple in Filipino cuisine and is often served as a main or side dish with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, chopped
  • 1/2 cup green beans, sliced
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 200g chicken or pork, sliced
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 35 minutes

Instructions

  1. In a pan, heat oil and saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken or pork and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook until pink. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add the vegetables (cauliflower, broccoli, carrots, green beans, cabbage, and bell peppers) and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add the cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce. Stir well.
  7. Return the shrimp to the pan and mix until heated through.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice.
