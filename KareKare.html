Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a traditional Filipino stew known for its rich and creamy peanut sauce. It is typically made with oxtail, tripe, or pork hock, and includes vegetables like eggplant, string beans, and banana blossoms. This dish is often served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) and steamed rice, making it a beloved comfort food in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail, tripe, or pork hock
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted rice
  • 2 tbsp annatto seeds (for coloring)
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup banana blossoms
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for serving

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 2 hours

Total Time: 2 hours 30 minutes

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail, tripe, or pork hock until tender. Set aside the broth.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add the annatto seeds to the pan and stir until the oil turns orange. Remove the seeds.
  4. Add the peanut butter and ground toasted rice to the pan. Gradually add the broth while stirring to create a thick sauce.
  5. Add the cooked meat to the sauce and simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Add the vegetables (eggplant, string beans, and banana blossoms) and cook until tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with bagoong and steamed rice.
