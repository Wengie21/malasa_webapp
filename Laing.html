Laing

Laing

Laing is a traditional Filipino dish made with dried taro leaves cooked in coconut milk, chili, and spices. This creamy and spicy dish is often paired with steamed rice and is a popular delicacy in the Bicol region of the Philippines. It is known for its rich flavor and unique texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried taro leaves
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 3-4 red chili peppers, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Instructions

  1. In a pot, saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the shrimp paste and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add the dried taro leaves, making sure they are fully submerged in the coconut milk. Do not stir to avoid releasing bitterness.
  5. Simmer on low heat until the taro leaves are tender and the coconut milk has thickened.
  6. Add the coconut cream and chili peppers. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
