Tokwa't Baboy

Tokwa't Baboy

Tokwa't Baboy is a popular Filipino appetizer made with deep-fried tofu (tokwa) and boiled pork, served with a tangy vinegar-soy sauce dip. This dish is a favorite pulutan (beer match) and is also enjoyed as a side dish or snack. Its savory and sour flavors make it a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly, boiled and sliced
  • 2 blocks firm tofu, cubed and deep-fried
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 red chili peppers, sliced (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Time

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 45 minutes

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork belly until tender. Let it cool and slice into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Deep-fry the tofu cubes until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, mix vinegar, soy sauce, onion, garlic, chili peppers (if using), sugar, salt, and pepper to make the dipping sauce.
  4. Combine the pork and tofu in a serving dish.
  5. Pour the dipping sauce over the pork and tofu, or serve it on the side.
  6. Serve as an appetizer, snack, or side dish.
